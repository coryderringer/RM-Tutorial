Practice and fatigue effects quiz


Please answer if the following statements are TRUE or FALSE.

Testing effects and practice effects are equivalent.

          

Correct answer: FALSE. A testing effect is a specifictype of practice effect. Practice effects occur when increasing time and experience in an experiment lead people to do better on later tasks. If this experience or practice they gain can be attributed to their exposure to the test being used as a measure, then it can be called a testing effect.

Fatigue effects can also occur in experiments using between-subjects designs.

          

Correct answer: TRUE. Fatigue effects occur when increasing time spent in an experiment decreases a participant's performance due to them becoming fatigued (having their resources depleted). This is likelier to occur in within-subject designs because participants have to experience more conditions, but is also possible in a between-subjects design. If participants have to experience a condition involving long/tiring/mundane procedures, they are likely to become fatigued by the end of the experiment, which could affect their scores on the dependent variable measure.

We can always tell when practice and fatigue effects are present from the results of our experiment.

          

Correct answer: FALSE. While unexpected results can give us some clues about the possible problems with our experimental procedures, we usually cannot conclude from the results alone that there were practice or fatigue effects. In our demonstrations, we used red and gray lines to represent important participant characteristics that were prone to these effects. However, it is unlikely we have independent measures of such characteristics in a real experiment. Usually, a mixture of past research and common sense will tell you if such effects are likely explanations for unexpected results. Finally, it is also entirely possible that your unexpected results are accurate, and that your theory made inaccurate predictions to begin with.
